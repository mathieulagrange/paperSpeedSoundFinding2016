Performance test of an audio graphical interface for sound data mining

Experiment instructions

The purpose is to successively find 13 target sounds in a sound bank using a web audio interface. All sounds are urban environmental sound events.

You have to find these 13 target sounds:

  • in the shortest amount of time

  • listening to the fewest sounds from the bank

The sound you have to find is strictly identical to the target sound.

The target sound is updated at each new search.

Use preferably your headphones for the experiment.

How do I use the web audio interface ?

  • Click on the button “Play Target Sound” to listen to the target sound. You can listen to the target sound at any time.

  • The sounds are depicted by circles. You can explore the sound data base by clicking on them. When a circle is selected it appears in bold.

  • Click on a circle to start the experiment. This action automatically triggers a timer and sets the “experiment state” to “In Progress”.

  • Click on the button “Found” when you have found the target sound. This action automatically stops the timer and sets the “experiment state” to “Pause”. A new target sound is then loaded. Click on the button “Play Target Sound” to listen to the new target sound. Click on a circle to begin a new search.

  • Find all the target sounds to end the experiment.

  • Click on the main red circle or rectangle to stop playing a sound.

How do I begin the experiment ?

1) From the email

  • Go to the experiment homepage by clicking on the corresponding link on the email.

  • You have to use Chrome to run the experiment.

2) From the experiment homepage

  • Click on the button “Check the volume” to check and adjust your headphones volume.

  • Click on the button “Generate the interface” to generate the interface. A link will pop-up.

  • Click on the link to be forwarded to the interface.

3) From the web audio interface

  • Enter your email address. It will remain strictly confidential and will not be used without the user's express permission.

  • Check the box if you wish to be informed of the experiment results.

  • Select your audio device.

  • Begin the experiment.

Warning

Use the time between two target sounds if you want to take a break. The timer stops when you click on “Found”.

Use Chrome or Firefox (version 18 or higher) to run the experiment.

Do not refresh or leave the web interface during the experiment. If you do so, do not restart the experiment.

Do not try to restart the experiment once you have finished.
